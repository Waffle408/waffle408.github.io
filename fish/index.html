Phish or Feline? — Spot the scam, save a cat

A tiny, silly demo to teach people how to spot phishing without feeding their paranoia. Includes cat memes as a reward for paying attention.

Example: a suspicious "urgent" email

From: IT Support <it-support@yourbank-login.com>
Subject: URGENT — Verify your account now or lose access
Dear user,

We detected unusual activity. Please verify your account immediately to avoid suspension.

Verify my account
Hover the link (or check the real link) — and don't click it here. This demo will safely analyze it for you.
Reward cat for reading. No cats were phished in the making of this demo.

Top red flags

Sender address looks off — it might say your bank, but the domain is wrong.
Pressure tactics — "urgent", "immediately", "suspend". Scammers pressure you to act without thinking.
Generic greeting — "Dear user" instead of your name can be a clue (not always).
Strange links or attachments — never open unexpected attachments or click weird links.
Legit logos and design can be faked — check sender, URL, and contact the organisation directly if unsure.
No scan yet — try pasting a link like http://login.bank-secure.example.com
